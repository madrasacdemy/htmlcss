Major Causes of Air Pollution

Air pollution stems from various human activities and natural sources. Understanding these causes is the first step toward finding solutions.

Industrial Emissions

Factories and power plants release large amounts of sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter into the atmosphere.

85% contribution to global emissions

Vehicle Emissions

Cars, trucks, and airplanes emit carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and other harmful pollutants that affect air quality.

75% of urban air pollution

Agricultural Activities

Farming practices release ammonia, methane, and other pollutants from fertilizers, livestock, and crop burning.

60% of methane emissions

Natural Sources

Wildfires, volcanic eruptions, and dust storms contribute significantly to air pollution, especially in certain regions.

40% from natural events

Household Sources

Burning solid fuels for cooking and heating releases harmful pollutants, particularly affecting indoor air quality.

55% in developing countries

Waste Management

Landfills and incineration of waste release methane, dioxins, and other toxic substances into the air.

45% of methane from waste

Devastating Effects of Air Pollution

The consequences of air pollution extend far beyond health issues, affecting our environment, economy, and future generations.

Health Impacts

Respiratory Diseases

Asthma, bronchitis, and lung cancer rates increase significantly in polluted areas.

Neurological Effects

Cognitive decline, developmental issues in children, and increased risk of dementia.

Cardiovascular Problems

Heart attacks, strokes, and high blood pressure linked to air pollution exposure.

Premature Deaths

1 in 9

deaths worldwide are attributed to air pollution

Climate Change

76%

of global greenhouse gas emissions contribute to climate change

Environmental Damage

Acid Rain

Damages forests, lakes, and buildings through chemical reactions in the atmosphere.

Ozone Depletion

Certain pollutants destroy the ozone layer, increasing UV radiation exposure.

Ecosystem Disruption

Affects plant growth, animal habitats, and biodiversity across ecosystems.
